Frequently asked questions

Absolutely. Unlike conventional team building activities, the treasure hunt creates genuine connection through shared challenges and a little healthy competition. There are no trust falls or awkward icebreakers — real camaraderie forms naturally as teams navigate Prague together, decode clues, and celebrate small wins. The experience is remembered long after the event.
All routes are done at a comfortable walking pace — there is no running, climbing, or physical challenge. We offer accessible route options for participants with mobility concerns. If someone in your group has specific requirements, let us know in advance and we will adapt the route accordingly. No one gets left behind.
The game is designed for groups of 9 to 300 participants. Smaller groups play as a single team; larger groups are automatically split into competing squads of 4–6 people. The sweet spot is 20–60 participants — in this range the dynamics are most lively. For groups over 100 people we deploy multiple Game Masters to maintain quality at the highest level.
All routes are designed to run in any weather and are conducted rain or shine. Light rain is no problem — many teams find it adds to the adventure. Your Game Master always has a contingency plan to move parts of the route under cover. In the event of truly extreme conditions (storm, lightning) we reschedule the event at no cost to a date of your choosing. We recommend bringing weather-appropriate clothing.
The active game runs for 2.5–3.5 hours depending on the route and difficulty level chosen. Add 15–20 minutes for the opening briefing at the start point and 20–30 minutes for the awards ceremony. Plan for roughly half a day in total. On request we can shorten the format to 2 hours or extend it to a full day with a lunch break — just mention it in your enquiry.

It depends on your team and preferences. Old Town is our most popular route — ideal for first-time visitors and international groups, rich in history and visual surprises. The Film Locations route is the perfect conversation-starter for cinephile teams. Not sure? We're happy to help you choose — just mention it in your enquiry.
During peak season (April–October), we recommend booking at least 3 weeks ahead. Popular slots — especially Fridays and groups over 50 — fill even earlier. Outside peak season we are considerably more flexible, and last-minute bookings with 1–2 weeks' notice are usually possible. The earlier you enquire, the greater your choice of available dates.
